<p><ul><li>Vapor Compression Systems</li><li>Cryogenic Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The chilled products transport market consists of two main types of refrigeration systems used to maintain cold temperatures during transportation. The first type is vapor compression systems, which utilize a compressor to condense and evaporate refrigerant, creating a cooling effect. These systems are commonly found in trucks and vans that transport perishable goods like fruits and vegetables. The second type is cryogenic systems, which involve the use of extremely low temperatures (below -150°C) using liquid nitrogen or carbon dioxide. These systems are prevalent in the transportation of highly sensitive items, such as pharmaceuticals and laboratory samples.</p></p>
