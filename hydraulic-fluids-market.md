<p><p>Hydraulic fluids are classified into different types based on their composition. Paraffinic oils are derived from petroleum and widely used due to their excellent oxidation stability. Naphthenic oils, also petroleum-based, are known for their superior low-temperature properties. Aromatic oils, derived from heavy petroleum fractions, offer high viscosity indexes but have limited applications due to their poor oxidation stability. Veg and bio oils, on the other hand, are renewable and environmentally friendly alternatives derived from vegetable sources. These oils are gaining popularity as they offer good biodegradability and lubricity.</p></p>
