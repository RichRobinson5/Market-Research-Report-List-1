<p><p>A fire resisting damper is a device used to prevent the spread of fire and smoke through ventilation ducts in buildings. It is typically installed within the ductwork and automatically closes when subjected to high temperatures, preventing the movement of flames and smoke from one compartment of the building to another. Fire resisting dampers play a crucial role in fire safety systems, ensuring the protection and evacuation of building occupants.</p><p>Currently, the fire resisting damper market is experiencing steady growth. This can be attributed to increasing regulations and stringent fire safety standards across various industries such as commercial buildings, healthcare, and manufacturing. The rising awareness about the importance of fire safety and the need to comply with safety regulations is driving the demand for fire resisting dampers.</p><p>Moreover, the growing construction industry, particularly in developing economies, is boosting the demand for fire resisting dampers.
