Linearly Polarized Light Sources in CD spectrometers emit light with a fixed polarization direction, while Circularly Polarized Light Sources emit light with continuously changing polarization direction. Multiple Light Sources in CD spectrometers provide a wider range of wavelengths and intensities for measurements. These different types of light sources enhance the versatility and accuracy of CD spectrometers in analyzing the structural and functional properties of molecules in pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and chemical industries.</p></p>
