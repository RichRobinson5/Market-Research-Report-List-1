<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are cylindrical nanostructures made up of carbon atoms. They have unique properties such as high electrical conductivity, excellent mechanical strength, and transparency, making them suitable for various applications. One of the most promising applications of CNTs is as transparent conductors in electronic devices such as touch screens, solar cells, and organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs).</p><p>Currently, indium tin oxide (ITO) is predominantly used as a transparent conductor in these devices. However, ITO has limitations such as brittleness, high production costs, and limited supply of indium, which has led to the exploration of alternative materials like CNTs. CNTs offer several advantages over ITO, including higher flexibility, improved durability, and better performance under extreme conditions.</p><p>The market for carbon nanotubes as transparent conductors is expected to grow significantly in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for touch screens in smartphones, tablets, and other electronic devices, as well as the growing popularity of OLED displays in the consumer electronics industry.
