<p><p>The porcine vaccine market consists of several types of vaccines. Inactivated vaccines contain killed viruses or bacteria, which stimulate an immune response. Live attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of the pathogen to trigger immunity. Toxoid vaccines contain inactivated toxins produced by the pathogen. Recombinant vaccines are made using genetic engineering techniques. Conjugate vaccines combine a part of the pathogen with a carrier protein. DNA vaccines use genetic material to stimulate an immune response. These different vaccine types provide various methods of protecting pigs from infectious diseases.</p></p>
