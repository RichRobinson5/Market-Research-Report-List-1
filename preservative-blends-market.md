There are several types of preservative blends available in the market. Parabens are chemically stable and widely used due to their effectiveness against a broad range of microorganisms. Formaldehyde-based blends release formaldehyde slowly, providing long-lasting protection. Halogenated blends contain chlorine or bromine compounds and are effective against bacteria and yeasts. Alcohols, such as ethanol and isopropanol, are commonly used as preservatives in cosmetics. Organic acid blends, like benzoic acid and sorbic acid, are natural alternatives. Other preservative blends include quaternary ammonium compounds and essential oils.</p></p>
