<p><p>Automotive chassis application sensors are used to monitor and control various aspects of a vehicle's chassis system. Two types of sensors commonly utilized in this context are electromagnetic suspension sensors and air suspension sensors. Electromagnetic suspension sensors are used in vehicles equipped with adjustable electromagnetic suspension systems to measure and regulate the suspension height and stiffness. On the other hand, air suspension sensors are employed in vehicles with air suspension systems to sense and maintain the desired vehicle height by monitoring air pressure and adjusting accordingly.</p></p>
