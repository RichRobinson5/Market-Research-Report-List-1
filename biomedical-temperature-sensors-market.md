<p><p>Biomedical temperature sensors are used in medical devices to measure body temperature accurately. Thermocouples are based on the principle of two different metals generating a small electrical voltage. Thermistors are temperature-sensitive resistors, which change their resistance based on temperature. Fibre optic sensors use optical fibers to measure temperature changes. Infrared sensors detect temperature by measuring the emitted infrared radiation. Liquid crystal temperature sensors change color based on temperature, providing a visual representation. These various types of sensors enable precise temperature monitoring in medical applications.</p></p>
