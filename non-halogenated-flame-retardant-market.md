<p><p>The non-halogenated flame retardant market is divided into three types: phosphorous, nitrogenous, and other. Phosphorous flame retardants inhibit the spread of fire by forming a protective barrier on the material's surface. Nitrogenous flame retardants work by releasing gases that dilute the combustible gases in a fire, reducing the flame's intensity. The "other" category includes flame retardants that do not fall under phosphorous or nitrogenous types. These flame retardants may use different mechanisms to prevent fire, such as cooling the material or preventing oxygen from reaching the flame.</p></p>
