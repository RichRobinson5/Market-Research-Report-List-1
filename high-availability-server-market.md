<p><p>The High-Availability Server market can be categorized into five different levels based on their availability: Level 1, Level 2, Level 3, Level 4, and Level 5. In Level 1, servers have basic redundancy measures but often experience downtime. Level 2 servers offer improved redundancy, reducing downtime significantly. Level 3 servers have multiple failover systems resulting in minimal downtime. Level 4 servers feature advanced failover mechanisms, providing near-continuous availability. Finally, Level 5 servers offer fault-tolerant architecture, ensuring uninterrupted operation, even during major disruptions.</p></p>
