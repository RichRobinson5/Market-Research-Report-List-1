<p><p>The agricultural secondary nutrients market includes three main types: calcium, magnesium, and sulfur. Calcium is essential for the development and strength of plant cell walls, helping with root development and disease resistance. Magnesium plays a crucial role in photosynthesis and enzyme activation, aiding in the production of chlorophyll and promoting overall plant growth. Sulfur contributes to protein synthesis and is vital for nitrogen-fixing bacteria in legumes. These secondary nutrients, along with primary nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, are necessary for optimal plant growth and vitality.</p></p>
