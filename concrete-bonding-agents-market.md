<p><p>Concrete bonding agents are used to enhance the adhesion between old and new concrete surfaces. There are two main types of concrete bonding agents: cementitious latex-based and epoxy-based. </p><p>Cementitious latex-based bonding agents are made from a combination of cement, latex polymers, and additives. They are effective in improving the bond strength and durability of concrete. These agents provide excellent adhesion properties and are commonly used in a variety of applications, including repairing damaged concrete or bonding new concrete to existing surfaces.</p><p>Epoxy-based bonding agents, on the other hand, consist of epoxy resins and hardeners. They offer superior bonding capabilities and are particularly suitable for demanding applications, such as heavy industrial environments or high-stress areas. Epoxy-based bonding agents provide exceptional chemical resistance and enhance the overall structural integrity of concrete. Their versatility allows for various applications, including bonding concrete to non-concrete substrates or repairing cracks in concrete structures.</p><p>Overall, the choice between cementitious latex-based and epoxy-based bonding agents depends on the specific requirements of the project, such as the level of adhesion required, the environmental conditions, and the desired durability.</p></p>
