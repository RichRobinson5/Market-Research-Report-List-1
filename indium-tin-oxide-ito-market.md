<p><p>Indium Tin Oxide (ITO) is a widely used transparent conducting material in various electronic devices and displays. The low temperature vacuum deposition type involves depositing ITO film onto a substrate at lower temperatures to prevent damage. The sputtering technique uses high-energy ions to dislodge atoms from an ITO target and deposit them onto the substrate. Electron beam evaporation involves heating an ITO source material with an electron beam, which then vaporizes and condenses onto the substrate. These methods are used to produce ITO films with high conductivity and optical transparency.</p></p>
