<p><p>An automotive supercapacitor, also known as an ultracapacitor, is an energy storage device that stores and releases electrical energy. <p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The automotive supercapacitor market comprises three main types: Electrochemical Double Layer Capacitor (EDLC), Pseudocapacitors, and Hybrid Capacitors. EDLC supercapacitors store energy through an electrostatic process, while Pseudocapacitors store energy by redox reactions. Hybrid Capacitors combine both the electrostatic and redox processes, making them suitable for high-power applications. EDLC supercapacitors are known for their high capacity, pseudocapacitors for their high energy density, and hybrid capacitors for providing a balance between energy density and power density. These various types cater to the diverse energy storage needs in the automotive industry.</p></p>
