<p><p>R134A refrigerant is produced using two different processes, namely the tetrachlorethylene process and the trichlorethylene process. In the tetrachlorethylene process, R134A is created by reacting 1,1,2-trichloroethane with hydrogen fluoride in the presence of a catalyst. On the other hand, the trichlorethylene process involves the reaction of trichloroethylene with hydrogen fluoride to produce R134A. Both processes are utilized in the market to manufacture R134A refrigerant, catering to the diverse demands of various industries that rely on this type of refrigerant.</p></p>
