<p><p>RF amplifier chips are integrated circuits designed to amplify radio frequency signals. There are two main types in the market - RF power amplifiers (PAs) and RF low noise amplifiers (LNAs). PAs are used to boost the power level of RF signals in transmission applications, such as in cell phones or wireless communication systems. LNAs, on the other hand, are utilized for receiving weak RF signals with minimum additional noise, commonly found in applications like satellite communication or radio receivers. These amplifier chips play a crucial role in enhancing signal strength and reducing noise for efficient RF transmission and reception.</p></p>
