<p><p>Rectennas, or rectifying antennas, serve as devices that convert radio frequency (RF) waves into direct current (DC) power. Different types of rectennas are available in the market, including array antennas, patch antennas, slot antennas, tag antennas, and dipole antennas. </p><p>Array antennas consist of multiple individual antennas, allowing for improved performance and increased gain. Patch antennas are flat, compact antennas that offer a wide range of applications due to their flexibility. Slot antennas feature narrow openings or slots for emitting and receiving RF signals. Tag antennas are designed specifically for RFID tags and enable wireless communication. Dipole antennas are simple, wire-based antennas that provide omnidirectional radiation patterns.</p></p>
