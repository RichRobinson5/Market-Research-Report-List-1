Mechanical valves are made of durable materials like metal and require lifelong blood-thinning medications. Both types of valves offer solutions for patients suffering from mitral valve diseases, but they differ in terms of materials and required follow-up care.</p></p>

<p><p>Transcatheter mitral valve repair and replacement is a medical procedure used to treat two types of conditions related to the mitral valve: mitral regurgitation and mitral valve stenosis. Mitral regurgitation is a condition in which the mitral valve does not close tightly enough, causing blood to leak backward into the heart. Mitral valve stenosis, on the other hand, is a condition where the mitral valve is narrowed, hindering the flow of blood from the left atrium to the left ventricle. Transcatheter techniques enable minimally invasive repair or replacement of the mitral valve, offering an alternative to open-heart surgery.</p></p>
