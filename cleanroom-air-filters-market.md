<p><p>Cleanroom air filters are a crucial component in maintaining high air quality within controlled environments. Two commonly used types are HEPA (High Efficiency Particulate Air) filters and ULPA (Ultra-Low Particulate Air) filters. HEPA filters are designed to capture and remove 99.97% of particles with a size of 0.3 micrometers or larger. ULPA filters, on the other hand, have a higher efficiency and can remove 99.999% of particles sized 0.12 micrometers or larger. These filters ensure clean and safe air by trapping and preventing the circulation of harmful particles, contaminants, and microorganisms.</p></p>
