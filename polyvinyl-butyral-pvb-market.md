<p><p>Polyvinyl butyral (PVB) is a polymer derived from the condensation reaction of butyraldehyde and polyvinyl alcohol. PVB finds application in various industries, one being the production of laminated safety glass. The two primary market types for PVB production are alkaline alcoholysis and acidic alcoholysis. Alkaline alcoholysis involves the reaction of polyvinyl alcohol with alkaline catalysts, while acidic alcoholysis involves the use of acid catalysts. PVB produced through alkaline alcoholysis offers enhanced performance and is widely preferred due to its superior mechanical properties, clarity, and adhesion strength compared to PVB produced through acidic alcoholysis.</p></p>
