<p><p>Semiconductor photoresist stripping refers to the process of removing photoresist materials from semiconductor wafers after the photolithography process. Positive photoresist stripping involves removing the photoresist that was exposed to light, leaving behind the desired pattern on the wafer. On the other hand, negative photoresist stripping removes the unexposed portion, leaving the desired pattern intact. Both types are used in semiconductor manufacturing to ensure accurate pattern transfer, but the specific choice depends on the requirements of the particular application.</p></p>
