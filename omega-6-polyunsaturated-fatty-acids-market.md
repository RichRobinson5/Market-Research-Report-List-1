<p><p>Omega-6 polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs) can be broadly categorized into two types: Linolenic Acid (LA) and Arachidonic Acid (AA). LA is an essential fatty acid found in plant-based oils and contributes to various physiological functions in the body. It is converted into other omega-6 fatty acids that help regulate inflammation and support the integrity of cell membranes. On the other hand, AA is derived from LA and is primarily found in animal-based products. AA plays a crucial role in brain development, immune function, and the synthesis of important signaling molecules called eicosanoids.</p></p>
