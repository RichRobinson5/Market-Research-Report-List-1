<p><p>Unmanned Underwater Vehicles (UUV) refer to vehicles that operate underwater without the need for human presence on board. There are two main types of UUV: Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV) and Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV). ROVs are controlled by an operator from the surface and are connected via cables, allowing real-time control and data transmission. On the other hand, AUVs are self-contained and operate autonomously without external control, using pre-programmed missions. Both ROVs and AUVs are used for various purposes such as underwater exploration, inspection, research, and military applications.</p></p>
