<p><p>Inorganic Metal Finishing refers to various processes used to enhance the surface properties of metal objects. Anodizing involves creating an oxide layer on the metal's surface, improving its corrosion resistance and durability. Cladding involves layering one metal onto another, providing additional protection or aesthetic appeal. Conversion coatings convert the metal surface into a protective layer. Electroplating involves depositing a thin metal layer onto a substrate, improving its appearance, conductivity, and corrosion resistance. Electroless plating is a similar process but does not require the use of external electrical current. Other types may include powder coating, passivation, and chemical etching.</p></p>
