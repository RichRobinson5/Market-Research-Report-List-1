<p><p>Label-free detection refers to a technique used in scientific research and diagnostics to detect biological interactions without the need for labeling or tagging molecules with fluorescent or radioactive markers. Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR) is a label-free detection technology that measures changes in the refractive index near a metal surface, allowing the real-time monitoring of molecular interactions. Bio-layer Interferometry (BLI) is another label-free detection method that measures the interference pattern of light reflected from a biosensor surface, enabling the quantitative analysis of biomolecular interactions.</p></p>
